Getting Your Project Across the Finish Line

For outdoor projects—landscapes, hardscapes, water features, drainage, irrigation—I handle the full scope from estimation through installation. I've managed $250,000+ in simultaneous projects with 27 people across four crews. I know how to keep things moving when the city is slow, materials are delayed, and stakes are high.

More importantly, I know how to keep everyone aligned—translating between architects, contractors, investors, and clients so nothing dies in miscommunication.

What This Includes

Project management isn't just about scheduling. It's about anticipating problems, maintaining communication, and ensuring quality at every stage.

  • Detailed estimation and budgeting—knowing what things actually cost before you commit
  • Crew coordination and scheduling—keeping multiple teams moving efficiently
  • Subcontractor management—working with trusted partners and holding them accountable
  • Quality control and site supervision—ensuring the work matches the plan
  • Client communication throughout—you always know where things stand
  • Problem-solving when things shift—because they always do

Experience
